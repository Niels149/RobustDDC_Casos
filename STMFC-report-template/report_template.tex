\section{Typesetting Formulas}

This is a brief summary of typographical conventions for technical/scientific writing based on DIN~1338. As a general rule of thumb, \textbf{variables and physical quantities are set in \textit{italic} font; everything else is set in upright font}. See \autoref{tab:TypesettingFormulas} and the following list for rules and examples.
\begin{table}[htbp]
	\centering
	\caption{Typesetting Formulas}
	\label{tab:TypesettingFormulas}
	\begin{tabular}{lll}
		\toprule
			\textbf{What?}	&	\textbf{How?}	&	\textbf{Examples}	\\
		\midrule
			Numbers denoted by digits	&	upright	&	1, 2, 3	\\
			Numbers denoted by symbols	&	\textit{italic}	&	$x, y, z, i, j, k$	\\
			Mathematical constants	&	upright	&	$\mathrm{e}, \uppi, \mathrm{j}$	\\
			Physical constants (e.g. speed of light)	&	\textit{italic}	&	$c$	\\
			Units (including their prefixes)	&	upright	&	m, km, \si{\micro\radian}	\\
			Indices follow the same convention	&	upright/\textit{italic}	&	$x_{\mathrm{true}} = \sum_{i=1}^n y_i$	\\
			Functions with arbitrary symbol	&	\textit{italic}	&	$f(x), g(u)$	\\
			Conventional functions	&	upright	&	$\sin, \cos, \ln, \exp$	\\
		\bottomrule
	\end{tabular}
\end{table}

\begin{itemize}
	\item Conventional functions and operators are separated from their arguments by a small space, e.g. $\sin\phi, \ln x$. Exceptions are the differential and difference operators, i.e. $\partial x$, $\td\phi$, and $\Delta t$. \LaTeX\ usually gets this right, so there should be no need to include \verb|\,| between function and argument.
	\item	Products are denoted by setting the factors next to each other ($x = yz$); potential ambiguities can be resolved by setting a small space with \verb|\,| ($x = y\,z$).
	\item Note the difference in German and English typesetting of numbers:
		\begin{gather*}
			\text{German: }	\num[locale = DE]{1,234e-5}	\\
			\text{English: }	\num[locale = US]{1,234e-5}
		\end{gather*}
		The \verb|\num| command of the \textsf{siunitx} package\footnote{\url{https://ctan.org/pkg/siunitx}} takes care of this: \verb|\num{0,12e3}| and \verb|\num{.12e3}| yield \num{0,12e3} and \num{.12e3}.
	\item Numbers and their units (except the degree symbol) are separated by a small space. The easiest way is to use the \textsf{siunitx} package: For instance, \verb|\qty{12345,67}{km}| and \verb|\qty{90}{\degree}| yield \qty{12345,67}{km} and \qty{90}{\degree}, respectively.
\end{itemize}
